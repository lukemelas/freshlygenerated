### Ingredients

* 1 cup blueberries

* 1 cup yellow seedless grapes

* 1 cup mini-pretzels

* 1 tablespoon or one 2-inch piece of finely shredded ginger

½ teaspoon ground cinnamon

* 1 garlic clove, peeled and minced

* 1 tablespoon brown sugar

Salt

½ teaspoon ground black pepper

* 4 thin cucumbers

* 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard

Salsa for serving
### Directions

1. Bring 1 quart of water to a boil in a small saucepan. Simmer the berries, grapes, pretzels, ginger, cinnamon, and garlic for 10 minutes, or until the fruit has softened. Remove from heat. Roughly chop, and set aside.

1. Heat the remaining ¼ cup of water in a small saucepan, then gently add the celery. Cook over medium heat, turning often, until it is tender, about 5 minutes. Add the dry ingredients and stir until all the dry ingredients are coated with the water.

1. Using a teaspoon or the back of a spoon, combine the cucumbers and blueberries in a bowl, tossing to combine. Toss in the celery and toss again.

1. Add the vinegar and honey, then season with salt and pepper. Scoop the mixture into the jars and squeeze over 1- to 2-inch chunks of salsa. Gently pour on additional salsa on top, and seal the jars.
